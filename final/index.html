When Jessie Ricci, a junior women, gender and sexuality studies major, heard that the university was making a cultural center for disabled students, she wanted to learn more and try to get involved.

Ricci, who identifies as disabled, found it difficult to find any information about the centers and what progress had been made. She then was made aware of an open forum discussion the Department of Student Affairs was hosting regarding the design through her disability justice class.

However when she got to the forum, she said she heard a staff member make a comment about being frustrated so many students were getting involved in the process.
