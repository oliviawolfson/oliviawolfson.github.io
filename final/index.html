In lower income Baltimore neighborhoods, there tend to be more liquor stores within walking distance rather than grocery stores, according to data from the city.

This contributes to the greater food desert problem that the city faces. The Baltimore City Health Department defines a food desert as an area more than a Â¼ mile distance to a supermarket, median household income at or below the federal poverty level and where 30% of households do not own a vehicle. The federal poverty line for a household of four is $27,750, according to ASPE.

For an area like Sandtown and Harlem Park, the average per capita income for 2020 was $16,694. There are nine liquor stores in these neighborhoods, some only a block away from each other, while there are no grocery stores.

The closest grocery for these residents is a Save A Lot in Upton, which is only a five minute drive. However 62.3% of households do not own a vehicle, according to the Baltimore Neighborhood Indicator Alliance, making the five minute drive a 20 minute walk.
